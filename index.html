This tool is a bedside aid for qualified clinicians and does not replace clinical judgement, local guidelines, or product information. Verify every figure independently. Reduce limits for elderly, frail, hepatic/cardiac disease, pregnancy, acidosis, or hypoalbuminaemia. You remain responsible for every dose you give.

Method. Additive fractional toxicity ∑(dose ÷ max dose) ≤ 1, with a user-selectable safety cap (80% default, 90%, or the theoretical maximum). Conservative adult mg/kg limits (plain solutions, lean body weight): lignocaine 4.5, bupivacaine 2, ropivacaine 3, levobupivacaine 2.

Caveats. Reduce further in elderly, frail, hepatic/cardiac disease, pregnancy, acidosis, hypoalbuminaemia, fascial plane blocks with large volumes, repeated blocks, or concomitant IV lignocaine infusion. The fractional model is intentionally simplistic — bupivacaine is substantially more cardiotoxic per mg than lignocaine. Bedside aid only; not a substitute for clinical judgement.

LAST — Lipid Rescue
Local Anaesthetic Systemic Toxicity · ASRA 2020 checklist · for adult patient
STEP 1 · IMMEDIATE
Stop LA injection. Call for help.
Airway: 100% O₂, manage seizures (benzodiazepine; avoid propofol if cardiovascular instability). Get lipid emulsion to bedside.
STEP 2 · LIPID 20%
Bolus
1.5 mL/kg lean body weight over 2–3 min
For this patient: enter weight first. Repeat bolus 1–2× for persistent cardiovascular collapse.
STEP 3 · INFUSION
Continuous
0.25 mL/kg/min
For this patient: enter weight first. Double to 0.5 mL/kg/min if BP remains low. Continue ≥10 min after circulatory stability. Maximum cumulative ≈12 mL/kg.
STEP 4 · CARDIAC ARREST
Modified ACLS
Reduce adrenaline to ≤1 µg/kg boluses. Avoid vasopressin, calcium channel blockers, β-blockers, lignocaine. Prolonged CPR may be needed — consider ECMO/cardiopulmonary bypass.
